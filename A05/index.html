About Romblon Island Philippines

Welcome to Romblon, the stunning "Marble Capital of the Philippines"! Nestled in the heart of the MIMAROPA region, Romblon is an enchanting island province made up of 20 islands, with Romblon Island, Tablas Island, and Sibuyan Island as its main gems. The provincial capital, Romblon town, is located on Romblon Island and serves as the gateway to this paradise. Spanning 1,036 square kilometers and home to around half a million residents, Romblon offers a vibrant cultural mix, where Tagalog, Hiligaynon, and English are spoken. A place with deep historical roots, Romblon has been famous for its marble quarries since the 16th century. Its premium marble has graced iconic landmarks, including the Manila Cathedral. But Romblon isn't just about marble—it's also a haven of natural beauty. Picture pristine white-sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, and lush, rolling landscapes. Whether you're looking for a peaceful getaway or an exciting island adventure, Romblon is the perfect destination—just a short ferry ride from Manila.

CALATRAVA ROMBLON

Located in the northwestern part of the Philippines, Romblon is one of the country's smallest provinces, but it has a lot to offer. Its capital town, also called Romblon, is considered as one of the most scenic municipalities in all of Luzon. It boasts both natural and man-made wonders that will surely leave you speechless.

More About Calatrava
TIAMBAN BEACH

Tiamban Beach in Romblon is a serene haven, known for its soft, powdery white sand and crystal-clear waters. It's a peaceful retreat away from the noise of daily life, perfect for families or anyone seeking relaxation. With its breathtaking sunsets and tranquil vibe, it's the ideal spot to unwind—just remember to pack your own snacks for a full day of enjoyment.

More About Tiamban
OCEAN'S EDGE RESORT

Located on Carabao Island, this peaceful resort features ocean-view villas, an infinity pool, and a private beach—ideal for relaxation and snorkeling.

More About Ocean's Edge
MOUNT GUITING-GUITING

Sibuyan Island’s highest peak, known for steep trails and rich biodiversity—earning it the nickname “Galapagos of Asia.” A protected natural park.

More About Mount Guiting-Guiting
FORT SAN ANDRES

Nestled amidst Romblon's natural beauty, Fort San Andres is a remarkable glimpse into the island's storied past. Beyond its famous marble deposits, Romblon is home to several heritage sites, and this fort stands out as one of the most significant. Built during the Spanish era, Fort San Andres boasts impressive architecture that has withstood over three centuries of history.

More About Fort San Andres
CORCUERA ROMBLON

Boasting its natural wonders and rich history, Corcuera is definitely a must-explore island in the province of Romblon. It is the perfect destination for those who want to reconnect with nature, as Corcuera has plenty of beaches and other hidden gems that will surely leave you in awe!
